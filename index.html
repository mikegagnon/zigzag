Many zigzag particles

Now there are many. They take turns moving. If one tries to move into a space with another, it doesn't move.

Lots of zigzag particles

Now there are lots. See how they cluster.

Detachable zigzag particles

Here, the behavior of zigzag particles changes. Specifically, if a zigzag particle tries and fails to move 100 times in a row, then it turns left, and starts over. This leads to dynamic clustering.
